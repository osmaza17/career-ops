# Mode: contact — LinkedIn Power Move

<purpose>
Draft targeted LinkedIn outreach messages (≤300 chars) for recruiters, hiring managers, peers, or interviewers at a target company — adapted by contact type.
</purpose>

<rules>
- Max 300 characters (LinkedIn connection request limit)
- No corporate-speak; no "I'm passionate about..."
- Never share a phone number
- Contact type changes EMPHASIS, not structure
- Never pitch a job to a Peer; let the referral happen naturally
- Always offer at least one alternative target with justification
</rules>

## Process

<process>

<step id="1" name="Identify targets">
<agent_instruction>
Search for:
- Hiring manager of the team
- Assigned recruiter
- 2–3 team peers (similar roles)
- Interviewer (if interview already scheduled)
</agent_instruction>
</step>

<step id="2" name="Classify contact type">
<agent_instruction>Ask the candidate or infer from context.</agent_instruction>

<reference id="contact-types">
| Type | Definition |
|------|------------|
| Recruiter | Talent acquisition, sourcing, or recruiting role |
| Hiring Manager | Person leading the hiring team |
| Peer | Similar role on the team — indirect referral path |
| Interviewer | Known interviewer with a scheduled date |
</reference>
</step>

<step id="3" name="Select primary target">
<agent_instruction>Choose the person who would benefit most from the candidate being there.</agent_instruction>
</step>

<step id="4" name="Generate message">
<agent_instruction>Apply the 3-sentence framework for the classified contact type.</agent_instruction>

<reference id="message-frameworks">

### Recruiter
- **S1 (Fit):** Direct match — role, relevant experience, availability or location
- **S2 (Proof):** Data point answering their screening questions before they ask (e.g. "5 years building ML pipelines, Berlin-based, available immediately")
- **S3 (CTA):** "Happy to share my CV if this aligns with what you're looking for"

### Hiring Manager
- **S1 (Hook):** Specific challenge facing their team (from JD, blog, or news)
- **S2 (Proof):** Candidate's most quantifiable achievement showing they've solved similar problems
- **S3 (CTA):** "Would love to hear how your team is approaching [specific challenge]"

### Peer (referral)
- **S1 (Interest):** Genuine reference to their work — blog post, talk, open source project, or publication
- **S2 (Connection):** Something the candidate is doing in the same space (NOT a job pitch)
- **S3 (CTA):** "I've been working on similar problems at [company], would love to hear your take on [topic]"

### Interviewer (pre-interview)
- **S1 (Research):** Reference to something specific about their work or trajectory
- **S2 (Context):** Light connection with the candidate's experience in that area
- **S3 (CTA):** "Looking forward to our conversation on [date]"
- Light tone — goal is for them to know you prepared, not to impress
</reference>
</step>

<step id="5" name="Language versions">
<agent_instruction>Produce EN by default; add ES version if the target company is Spanish-speaking.</agent_instruction>
</step>

<step id="6" name="Alternative targets">
<agent_instruction>Suggest at least one alternative contact with a brief justification for why they are a strong second choice.</agent_instruction>
</step>

</process>

<review_loop>
Present the draft message(s) to the candidate. Iterate until approved — do not send or log anything without explicit confirmation.
</review_loop>
